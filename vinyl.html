Frequently Asked Questions

We Answer Your Questions

What is vinyl flooring?

+

Vinyl flooring is a synthetic floor covering made from PVC (polyvinyl chloride) that offers durability, water resistance, and easy maintenance. It's available in sheets, planks, or tiles, and mimics the appearance of natural materials like wood, stone, or tile.

What is the difference between vinyl flooring and vinyl plank flooring?

+

Vinyl flooring refers to all types of vinyl floor coverings, including sheets, tiles, and planks. Vinyl plank flooring, specifically, is designed to resemble hardwood planks, providing the same aesthetic with the added benefits of being waterproof, durable, and low maintenance.

What type of vinyl flooring is best for the office?

+

The best vinyl flooring for an office is one that is durable, easy to clean, and water-resistant. Luxury vinyl planks (LVP) or tiles (LVT) are ideal due to their resilience, variety of designs, and ease of maintenance. Look for options with a commercial-grade wear layer for long-term performance in high-traffic areas.

How can vinyl flooring benefit my commercial space?

+

Vinyl flooring offers numerous advantages for commercial spaces, including durability, water resistance, and low maintenance. It’s also sound-absorbing, reducing noise levels, and comes in various styles, allowing businesses to choose a design that complements their branding or interior design.

What materials does Nestora use for vinyl flooring?

+

Nestora uses high-quality luxury vinyl tiles (LVT) and planks (LVP) that are scratch-resistant, waterproof, and made from environmentally friendly PVC. These materials ensure a long-lasting, practical, and aesthetically pleasing solution for both residential and commercial spaces.

What vinyl flooring is best for my residential space?

+

For residential spaces, luxury vinyl planks (LVP) or tiles (LVT) are ideal due to their durability, easy maintenance, and wide variety of styles. Choose a design that complements your home’s aesthetic, whether you're looking for the look of wood, stone, or tile. Consider adding options with a textured finish for added comfort.
