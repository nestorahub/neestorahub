Frequently Asked Questions

We Answer Your Questions

What is parquet flooring?

+

Parquet flooring is a type of hardwood flooring made from small, individual pieces of wood arranged in geometric patterns. It offers a classic, elegant look and is often used to create unique, eye-catching designs such as herringbone or chevron patterns.

What is the difference between parquet flooring and hardwood flooring?

+

Parquet flooring is a specific type of hardwood flooring where the wooden pieces are arranged in a pattern, whereas traditional hardwood flooring consists of long, straight planks of wood. Parquet offers more design versatility, while hardwood flooring tends to have a more uniform, natural appearance.

What type of parquet flooring is best for the office?

+

The best parquet flooring for an office is one that is durable and easy to maintain, such as engineered wood parquet. Engineered parquet flooring combines a hardwood veneer with a plywood base, making it more stable and resistant to changes in temperature and humidity, ideal for commercial spaces.

How can parquet flooring benefit my commercial space?

+

Parquet flooring adds a sophisticated and timeless look to any commercial space. It’s known for its durability, resistance to wear, and ease of maintenance. The intricate patterns also create a unique aesthetic, making it perfect for offices, lobbies, or any area that requires both style and function.

What materials does Nestora use for parquet flooring?

+

Nestora uses high-quality engineered hardwood for its parquet flooring, ensuring durability and stability. The top layer of the flooring is made from solid wood, which is available in a variety of finishes, while the core layer is made from plywood or MDF for additional strength and resistance to moisture.

What parquet flooring is best for my residential space?

+

For residential spaces, solid wood parquet flooring works well in living rooms, dining rooms, or entryways, offering a timeless aesthetic. Engineered parquet is also an excellent option for areas with fluctuating temperatures or humidity levels, such as basements or kitchens. Choose a finish that matches your home's decor, whether you prefer a natural wood look or a darker, more modern finish.
